RGB Color Model

RGB (an abbreviation of the English words red, green, blue) is a color model that describes the way color is encoded in electronic devices. In the RGB model, three colors are used to reproduce colors, which are commonly called primary:

  • Red
  • Green
  • Blue

The choice of these particular colors is related to the of the structure of the human retina. In the retina, three types of cones—receptors responsible for color perception—have been found. Each type of receptor perceives only one of the three colors of the RGB model.
